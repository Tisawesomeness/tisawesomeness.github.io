### Physics

Sulfur Cubes now use the following process when hit by an attack:
1. Find the horizontal angle between the cube's center and the player's aim direction and expand it by 60%. For example, if the player is looking 5 degrees to the right of the cube's center, the cube will be knocked back 8 degrees to the right.
2. Scale horizontal knockback from 50% when looking at the bottom of the cube, to 150% when looking at the top of the cube.
3. Scale vertical knockback from 150% when looking at the bottom of the cube, to 50% when looking at the top of the cube.
4. Rotate the `<horizontalKnockbackPower, verticalKnockbackPower>` vector vertically by the vertical angle from the player's position to the cube's position. For example, if the player is below the cube, the cube will be knocked back more vertically and less horizontally.
5. Scale knockback power by the square root of damage taken. An attack that does no damage will deal no knockback.
6. Scale knockback power by `1 - knockbackResistance`. A knockback resistance of -2 will make the cube get knocked back twice as far.
7. Multiply horizontal knockback by 0.4 and vertical knockback by 1.2

Sulfur Cube knockback is no longer affected by their size or scale.

## Mobs

Wandering Traders now sell up to 5 trades of 2 Sulfur Spikes for 1 emerald.

## Potent Sulfur

Geysers now emit a vibration with frequency **10** when they start erupting and a vibration with frequency **9** when they stop erupting.

## Data

A Sulfur Cube archetype with `explosion` that is primed by an explosion will explode after a random number of ticks between:

$$ \max\left( 1, \left\lfloor \frac{f}{8} \right\rfloor \right) \quad \text{to} \quad \max\left( 1, \left\lfloor \frac{f}{8} \right\rfloor + \left\lfloor \frac{f}{4} \right\rfloor - 1 \right) $$

Where `f` is the `fuse` of the explosion.
